LIFE BELOW WATER

Life below water is vital for regulating climate, producing oxygen, and supporting biodiversity. Oceans provide food, medicine, and livelihoods while protecting coastal communities from storms. However, pollution, overfishing, and climate change threaten these ecosystems, making their preservation crucial for the planet's health and human survival.

BIODIVERSITY PRESERVATION

Food Web Stability

Marine species are interdependent. If one species is lost, it can disrupt the entire food chain, leading to the collapse of ecosystems.

Medical Resources

Marine biodiversity provides a source of important pharmaceuticals, such as cancer-fighting compounds derived from coral reefs and sponges.

Ecosystem Services

Marine habitats like coral reefs and mangroves protect coastlines from erosion and flooding, ensuring safe human settlements.

Global Food Security

The preservation of fish species ensures sustainable fisheries, which feed over 3 billion people worldwide.

SUPPORT OF HUMAN LIVELIHOODS

Fisher and Aquaculture

Over 120 million people globally depend on fishing and aquaculture for their livelihoods Oceans provide 16% of the world's animal protein, making sustainable fishing essential for good security.

Tourism and Recreation

Coral reefs, marine parks, and pristine beaches attract millions of Tourism each year, generating bullions of dollars in revenue and creating jobs for local communities.

Raw Materials and Trade

Oceans are a source of valuable resources, including oil, natural gas, and mu]inerals, and facilitate 90% of global trade through shipping

CLIMATE REGULATION

Stabilization of Global Temperatures

Climate regulation helps maintain balanced global temperatures, ensuring conditions suitable for ecosystems, agriculture, and human livelihoods. Without proper regulation, extreme heat or cold could disrupt food systems, biodiversity, and human health.

Support for Ecosystems and Biodiversity

Stable climate systems sustain diverse ecosystems by maintaining predictable weather patterns and resource availability. Many species depend on specific temperature ranges and seasonal cycles for survival, and disruptions can lead to biodiversity loss.

Protection from Extreme Weather Events

Effective climate regulation reduces the frequency and severity of extreme weather events like hurricanes, droughts, and floods. This minimizes damage to infrastructure, prevents loss of life, and reduces economic disruptions.
